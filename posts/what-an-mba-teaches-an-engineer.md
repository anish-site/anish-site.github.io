# What an MBA Teaches an Engineer

A few months into the Post Graduate Program in Management at Great Lakes, the
biggest surprise isn't the content — it's how differently you're asked to *think*.

## Engineers optimize. Managers allocate.

As an engineer, the instinct is to find the correct answer and build it well.
Business school keeps asking a harder question: **given limited money, time, and
people, what's the right bet?** There's rarely one correct answer — only trade-offs
you can defend.

## Three things that have reframed how I work

- **Finance is a language, not a hurdle.** Once you can read a P&L, a lot of
  "business decisions" stop feeling arbitrary.
- **Strategy is about saying no at the company level.** Same muscle as a roadmap,
  bigger stakes.
- **The hardest skill is simplicity.** Explaining something complex in one clear
  slide is far harder than building the complex thing.

## What I'm carrying back to product

Engineering taught me how systems work. The MBA is teaching me why they matter to
the people paying for them. Put together, that's the kind of product leader I want
to be — fluent in both the build and the business.
